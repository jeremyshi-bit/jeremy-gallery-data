Mar 02, 2018

Spring With Flowers

Is an APS-C 135mm enough to photograph sakura in the park?

Under bright daylight, somewhere between f/5 and f/7, I walked among the trees looking for a different way to see a familiar subject. The classic approach is well known—wide aperture, soft focus, backgrounds melting into pastel blur. Beautiful, yes, but predictable.

I also wanted something else.

Something with a bit more distance, more structure. To let the branches breathe, to let the light carve the petals instead of dissolving them.

With the 135mm, every step became deliberate.

Framing tightened, layers shifted, and the flowers began to feel less like decoration and more like part of a larger scene—spring unfolding, people passing, wind brushing through the canopy.

A lens that usually compresses the world suddenly offered a quiet, cinematic way to see it.

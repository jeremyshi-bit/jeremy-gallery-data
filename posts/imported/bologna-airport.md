Jul 30, 2017

Bologna Airport

Bologna Guglielmo Marconi Airport—named after the man who sent the first telegraph across the world.

A modest-sized hub in northern Italy, but full of small details that speak the language of the country: design, utility, and a certain effortless charm.

On the apron, the TGV—taxi guiding vehicle—was a Fiat 500, unmistakably Italian in scale and personality. It rolled across the concrete with a kind of casual confidence, its tiny silhouette framed against the wide expanse of runway.

Farther out, a blue Lamborghini tractor waited—likely for pushback duty—its industrial presence forming a quiet contrast to the sports cars that share the same badge. And beyond that, nearly blending into the haze, an IVECO Stralis 400 carried an aero refueling tank, completing a lineup of machines that turned the tarmac into a layered portrait of Italian engineering.

A simple airport moment, but one where the machinery tells its own story—functional, iconic, and unmistakably tied to the character of the place.
